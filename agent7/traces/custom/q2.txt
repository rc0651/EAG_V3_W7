Based on the provided regulatory documentation, brokers face specific obligations and consequences regarding margin collection:

1.  **Mandatory Margin Collection:** Brokers are required to ensure that the margin collected from clients at the time of intraday snapshots (performed at least four times daily) is at least equal to the "peak margin" obligation.
2.  **Risk Mitigation and Square-off:** To manage the risks associated with insufficient margins, brokers are authorized to square off client positions. This process is designed to minimize market impact while prioritizing risk reduction, and it does not require additional client consent at the time of the action, as the policy is pre-consented during account opening.
3.  **Reporting and Accountability:** Brokers must submit daily reports to exchanges and clearing corporations detailing client-wise margin requirements and the actual margin collected. Failure to adhere to these reporting and collection standards exposes the broker to regulatory scrutiny, particularly regarding defaults that may arise if client position losses exceed the margins actually collected.
