FINAL ANSWER:
Claude Shannon, widely regarded as the "father of information theory," was born on April 30, 1916, and passed away on February 24, 2001.

His three key contributions to information theory include:

1.  **Founding Information Theory:** In his landmark 1948 paper, "A Mathematical Theory of Communication," he established the mathematical framework for digital communication, defining the fundamental problem of communication as the accurate reproduction of a message from one point to another.
2.  **Introduction of the "Bit":** He introduced the "bit" (binary digit) as the basic unit of information, which allowed for the consistent measurement and management of data across various fields, including computer science and physics.
3.  **Noisy-Channel Coding Theorem:** He proved that reliable communication is possible over a noisy channel as long as the information rate remains below the channel's capacity, a discovery that laid the groundwork for modern error detection and correction techniques used in digital storage and transmission.
