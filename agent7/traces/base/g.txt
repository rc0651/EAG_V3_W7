This ensures that excess funds held by the broker are returned to the client, reducing the window for potential misuse.
2.  **Direct Collateral Pledging:** To prevent brokers from misusing client securities, the current process mandates that clients pledge their securities directly into the depository system. This removes the broker as an intermediary holder of the collateral, ensuring the assets remain under the client's control.
3.  **Strict Margin Frameworks:** SEBI enforces rigorous margin requirements (such as VaR margins and peak margin frameworks) to ensure that brokers collect adequate collateral for open positions. By mandating these collections, SEBI ensures that brokers are not using their own capital or other clients' funds to cover losses arising from a specific client's position.
4.  **KYC and Oversight:** Brokers are obligated to perform thorough Know Your Customer (KYC) procedures for every client. This foundational step ensures that all financial activity is traceable and linked to verified identities, which is essential for monitoring compliance and preventing the commingling of funds.
