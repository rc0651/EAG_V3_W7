Based on the regulatory guidelines prescribed by SEBI and the stock exchanges, the main obligations of a stockbroker towards their clients include the following:

1.  **Protection of Client Interests:** A stockbroker is obligated to ensure the protection of the client's rights regarding their investments, including dividends, bonus shares, and rights issues. They must not engage in any actions that could harm the client's interests and must act with fairness and competence in all dealings.
2.  **Communication and Transparency:** Stockbrokers must provide timely and accurate communication, such as issuing contract notes (in physical or electronic form) for all transactions. Additionally, they are required to inform clients about the features, risks, responsibilities, and liabilities associated with various trading technologies, such as internet-based or wireless trading.
3.  **Grievance Redressal and Compliance:** Stockbrokers must cooperate in resolving client grievances regarding transactions routed through them. They are also required to maintain proper records, settle accounts periodically in accordance with SEBI/Exchange regulations, and adhere to all statutory rules, bylaws, and circulars issued by regulatory authorities.
