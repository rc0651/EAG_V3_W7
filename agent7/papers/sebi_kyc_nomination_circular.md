# SEBI Circular on KYC Norms and Nomination Requirements for Securities Market Participants

**Circular Reference:** SEBI/HO/MIRSD/MIRSD-PoD-1/P/CIR/2023/058 and SEBI/HO/MIRSD/MIRSD-PoD-1/P/CIR/2024/12
**Date:** April 6, 2023 (original); February 14, 2024 (updated provisions)
**Issued By:** Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI)
**Applicable To:** All SEBI-Registered Intermediaries, KYC Registration Agencies (KRAs), Stock Exchanges, Depositories

---

## 1. Introduction

The Know Your Customer (KYC) process is fundamental to the prevention of money laundering, terrorist financing, and market manipulation in the securities markets. SEBI has progressively strengthened KYC requirements over the years, and this circular consolidates and updates the key requirements for KYC as well as introduces mandatory nomination requirements for securities accounts.

---

## 2. KYC — Mandatory Documents and Verification

### 2.1 Officially Valid Documents (OVDs) for Identity and Address Proof

Clients must provide the following documents as part of KYC:

**Identity Proof (any one):**
- Passport
- Driving Licence issued by the Regional Transport Authority
- Voter ID Card (EPIC) issued by the Election Commission of India
- Aadhaar Card issued by UIDAI (subject to Aadhaar authentication)
- National Population Register (NPR) Letter (for Indian citizens)
- NREGA Job Card (with photograph)

**Address Proof (any one):**
- Any of the above OVDs if they contain a current address
- Utility bill (electricity, telephone, gas) not older than 3 months
- Bank account statement not older than 3 months
- Ration card with photograph
- Employer-certified accommodation letter (for employees in government or large organisations)

Aadhaar-based eKYC (electronic KYC using biometric authentication through UIDAI) is permitted as a complete KYC solution when conducted with the client's explicit consent and with Aadhaar authentication at point of service. Aadhaar eKYC reduces the need for physical document submission and speeds up account opening.

### 2.2 PAN Card — Mandatory for All Clients

The Permanent Account Number (PAN) is mandatory for all market participants transacting in securities markets in India. No new trading, demat, or mutual fund account may be opened without a valid PAN. The following transactions in securities markets require PAN:

- Opening a trading account with a stock broker
- Opening a demat account with a depository participant
- Any purchase or sale of securities above Rs. 50,000 in a single transaction
- Any mutual fund investment above Rs. 50,000 per transaction
- All F&O trades regardless of value

PAN must be verified by the intermediary against the Income Tax Department's database (ITD-PAN verification) before accepting it as valid KYC documentation.

### 2.3 In-Person Verification (IPV)

All new clients must undergo In-Person Verification (IPV) before their accounts are activated. IPV may be conducted:

- Physically, at the broker's office or at the client's location (by a representative of the broker)
- Digitally, through a video-based IPV (V-IPV) using the client's smartphone camera with geo-tagging and liveness detection

During V-IPV, the client must:
- Show their original PAN card to the camera
- Perform a liveness check (blink, smile, or head movement as prompted)
- Read a randomly generated code displayed on screen

V-IPV records must be stored securely for a minimum of 5 years and must be available for inspection.

### 2.4 KYC Upload to KYC Registration Agencies (KRAs)

After completing KYC, intermediaries must upload the client's KYC data to a SEBI-registered KRA (currently CAMS KYC, KFINTECH, CSDL Ventures, NSDL Database Management, and Karvy) within 10 working days of account opening.

The benefit of KRA upload is that KYC completed with one intermediary is recognised by all other SEBI-registered intermediaries. A client who is KYC-compliant with one broker does not need to re-submit documents when opening an account with another broker; instead, the new broker retrieves the KYC record from the KRA.

## 3. Periodic KYC Update Requirements

### 3.1 Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Renewal

Intermediaries must conduct periodic re-KYC (Customer Due Diligence renewal) for all clients. The frequency of re-KYC depends on the client's risk classification:

- **High-Risk Clients:** Re-KYC at least once every 2 years
- **Medium-Risk Clients:** Re-KYC at least once every 8 years
- **Low-Risk Clients:** Re-KYC at least once every 10 years

High-risk clients include Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs), clients with adverse media mentions, clients with significant cash transactions, and non-resident Indian (NRI) clients.

Re-KYC requires the client to resubmit current documentation and verify that their address, contact details, income details, and other KYC information remain accurate.

### 3.2 Consequences of Non-Compliance with Re-KYC

If a client fails to complete re-KYC within the prescribed period after being notified by the intermediary:

- The intermediary must place a "hold" on the client's account, preventing new purchases or new transactions (but permitting existing positions to be squared off or redeemed).
- If the client fails to complete re-KYC within 30 days of the hold being placed, the intermediary must report the non-compliance to the relevant exchange and the Financial Intelligence Unit.
- The intermediary may not freeze existing demat holdings solely on account of KYC non-compliance, but may restrict further purchases.

### 3.3 Account Modification Triggers

The following events require the client to update their KYC information within 30 days:

- Change of name (due to marriage, court order, or other reasons)
- Change of address
- Change of contact details (mobile number, email address)
- Change of income bracket
- Change of nationality or residential status (e.g., becoming an NRI)

## 4. Nomination Requirements — New Mandatory Provisions

### 4.1 Background on Nomination

Nomination allows a securities account holder to designate one or more nominees who will receive the securities or funds held in the account in the event of the account holder's death. Prior to SEBI's strengthened nomination rules, many accounts were held without any nomination, creating significant complications for legal heirs who had to go through lengthy legal processes to obtain securities after the account holder's death.

### 4.2 Mandatory Nomination or Opt-Out Declaration

With effect from March 31, 2023, all existing individual demat account holders and trading account holders must either:

- **Provide Nomination:** Register at least one and up to three nominees for their demat account. Nominees may be individuals, minor (with a guardian designated), or religious/charitable trusts.
- **Opt Out of Nomination:** Submit a declaration explicitly opting out of nomination, acknowledging that they have made alternative arrangements for transmission of securities.

Accounts for which neither nomination nor opt-out declaration is received as of the deadline will have debits (sale transactions) frozen until the client complies. Credits (purchase transactions and dividend credits) will continue to be permitted. Debit freeze means the client cannot sell, pledge, or transfer securities from the frozen demat account.

New accounts opened after the effective date of this circular must provide nomination or opt-out declaration at the time of account opening. The trading or demat account will not be activated without completion of the nomination process.

### 4.3 Procedure for Adding Nominees

Nomination can be registered through:

- Physical nomination form submitted to the depository participant
- Online nomination through the DP's portal using Aadhaar OTP authentication
- Through the intermediary's mobile app with SEBI-prescribed authentication

The nomination form must contain:
- Full name and address of the nominee(s)
- Relationship with the account holder
- Date of birth of nominee (mandatory if nominee is a minor)
- Name and address of guardian (if nominee is a minor)
- Percentage share of each nominee (if multiple nominees are registered, must total 100%)
- Photograph of nominee (optional but recommended)

### 4.4 Transmission of Securities to Nominees

Upon the death of a demat account holder:

- Nominees (or legal heirs if no nomination) must submit a death certificate, nomination certificate, and KYC documents to the DP.
- For accounts with nomination and assets below Rs. 15 lakhs, the DP may transfer securities to the nominee within 30 days without requiring a succession certificate or probate.
- For accounts with assets above Rs. 15 lakhs without nomination, a succession certificate, probate, or letters of administration is required.

## 5. Contact Detail Verification — Mobile and Email

SEBI has mandated that all client accounts must have a verified mobile number and email address linked to them. This requirement serves dual purposes: it facilitates electronic delivery of contract notes and account statements, and it provides a secure second factor for authentication and alerts.

### 5.1 Mandatory Mobile and Email Registration

All clients must provide:
- A unique mobile number (not shared with any other client account)
- A valid email address (the same email may be used for multiple accounts within the same family, but preferably unique)

Mobile numbers and email addresses must be verified via OTP before being recorded in the system.

### 5.2 System-Triggered Alerts

Intermediaries must send automatic electronic alerts to clients for the following events:

- Each trade executed (within 5 minutes of execution)
- Each credit or debit to the client's funds account above Rs. 10,000
- Each login to the trading platform from a new device
- Each change to account-level settings (password, bank account, mobile number)
- Each pledge or unpledge of securities

## 6. KYC Compliance for Corporate Clients

For corporate clients (companies, LLPs, trusts, and other non-individual entities), KYC requirements include:

- Certificate of Incorporation and Memorandum & Articles of Association
- Board Resolution authorising the account opening and specifying authorised signatories
- KYC of all directors and beneficial owners holding more than 25% stake
- Certified copy of list of shareholders/partners holding more than 25% stake
- CKYC (Central KYC Registry) registration for NBFCs and regulated entities
- UBO (Ultimate Beneficial Owner) declaration as per PMLA Rules

Corporate clients must re-KYC every 2 years regardless of their risk classification.

---

## 7. Conclusion

The KYC and nomination framework represents SEBI's commitment to both investor protection and financial system integrity. Robust KYC prevents bad actors from using securities markets for illicit purposes, while mandatory nomination ensures that legitimate investors' families are not burdened with prolonged legal processes upon the investor's death. Intermediaries who implement these requirements diligently contribute to a more transparent, trustworthy, and investor-friendly securities market.
