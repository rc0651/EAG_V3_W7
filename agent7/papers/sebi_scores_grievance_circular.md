# SEBI SCORES 2.0 — Investor Grievance Redressal System

**Circular Reference:** SEBI/HO/OIAE/IGRD/CIR/P/2023/156
**Date:** September 20, 2023
**Issued By:** Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI)
**Applicable To:** All SEBI-Registered Intermediaries, Stock Exchanges, Depositories, Asset Management Companies, Mutual Fund Distributors, and Other Market Participants

---

## 1. Background — The SEBI Complaints Redress System (SCORES)

SEBI established the SCORES (SEBI Complaints Redress System) platform in June 2011 as a centralised, online system through which investors can lodge complaints against SEBI-registered entities. Over more than a decade, SCORES has become the primary interface for investor grievance redressal in Indian securities markets. As of March 2023, SCORES had received over 6 million complaints since inception, with an average of approximately 35,000–40,000 new complaints per month.

SCORES 2.0 represents a comprehensive upgrade of the platform, incorporating lessons from over a decade of operation to enhance complaint resolution speed, transparency, and quality. This circular describes the key features of SCORES 2.0 and the obligations of intermediaries under the upgraded framework.

---

## 2. Key Features of SCORES 2.0

### 2.1 Designation of Nodal Officers

Every SEBI-registered intermediary must designate:

- A **Nodal Officer** (senior management level, not below the rank of Executive Vice President or equivalent) who is accountable for SCORES complaint resolution and is the primary point of contact for SEBI on SCORES matters.
- A **Deputy Nodal Officer** who handles day-to-day complaint management on SCORES.

The Nodal Officer must be registered on the SCORES 2.0 portal within 30 days of this circular. The Nodal Officer cannot delegate SEBI responses on SCORES to junior staff without supervision.

### 2.2 Auto-Routing of Complaints

SCORES 2.0 incorporates automated routing of complaints to the relevant intermediary based on the complainant's details and the nature of the complaint. This eliminates delays previously caused by manual routing. The routing algorithm:

- Identifies the relevant intermediary from the complainant's account details.
- Assigns the complaint to the intermediary's queue in SCORES.
- Sends an automatic acknowledgement to the complainant with a unique complaint registration number and the expected resolution timeline.

### 2.3 Two-Level Complaint Resolution — Timelines

SCORES 2.0 operates on a two-level resolution model:

**Level 1 — Intermediary Resolution:**
The complaint is first sent to the relevant SEBI-registered intermediary (e.g., stock broker, depository participant, mutual fund, or listed company). The intermediary must:
- Acknowledge the complaint to the complainant within **3 working days** of the complaint being assigned.
- Provide a resolution or substantive update to the complainant and on SCORES within **21 calendar days** of the complaint being assigned.

If the intermediary resolves the complaint to the complainant's satisfaction within 21 days, the complaint is marked as "Resolved."

**Level 2 — SEBI Designated Body Review:**
If the complainant is not satisfied with the intermediary's resolution, they may escalate the complaint to Level 2 within 15 days of receiving the intermediary's response. Level 2 review is conducted by a SEBI-designated body (stock exchange or other SRO appointed by SEBI). At Level 2:
- The designated body reviews both the complaint and the intermediary's response.
- The designated body may call for additional information from either party.
- The designated body must provide a decision within **21 calendar days** of the escalation.

If the complainant is still not satisfied after Level 2, they may approach SEBI directly. SEBI will consider the full history before deciding on enforcement action or conciliation.

### 2.4 Deemed Closure

If the complainant does not respond within 15 days of the intermediary providing a resolution, the complaint may be marked as "Deemed Closed." However:

- Deemed closure does not prevent the complainant from subsequently escalating to Level 2.
- The deemed closure window is 15 days from the date the resolution is uploaded by the intermediary.
- Intermediaries must not exert any pressure on clients to close complaints prematurely.

## 3. Obligations of Intermediaries on SCORES 2.0

### 3.1 Timely and Quality Responses

The quality of responses on SCORES is monitored by SEBI. Intermediaries must:

- Provide specific, factual responses to each grievance. Generic "complaint is not maintainable" or "client is not entitled to any relief" responses without supporting evidence will be treated as inadequate and may attract penalties.
- Upload all relevant supporting documents (trade confirmations, fund transfer records, correspondence logs) with the SCORES response.
- Ensure that the resolution offered in the SCORES response matches what has actually been communicated to the complainant.

### 3.2 Escalation to Senior Management

Complaints involving large financial claims (above Rs. 1 lakh) or complaints from institutional investors must be reviewed by a senior manager (Vice President level or above) before the SCORES response is uploaded. The reviewing officer's name and designation must be mentioned in the SCORES response.

### 3.3 Complaint Reduction Programme

Intermediaries with a high volume of complaints on SCORES (above 10 complaints per 10,000 active clients per month) will be placed on SEBI's "Complaint Monitoring Programme." Under this programme:

- The intermediary must submit a root-cause analysis report to SEBI within 30 days.
- The intermediary must submit a corrective action plan and implement it within 90 days.
- SEBI will conduct an on-site inspection of the intermediary's complaint handling processes within 6 months.
- Persistent non-improvement (complaint rate remaining above threshold for 3 consecutive quarters) may result in suspension of certificate of registration.

## 4. Investor Protection — ODR (Online Dispute Resolution) Integration

### 4.1 The SEBI Conciliation and Arbitration Framework

Alongside SCORES, SEBI has strengthened the Online Dispute Resolution (ODR) mechanism, which allows investors to seek conciliation or arbitration for disputes with intermediaries without going to court. ODR is facilitated through SEBI-accredited ODR providers.

**Conciliation Process:**
- The investor and intermediary are brought together (online) before a neutral conciliator accredited by SEBI.
- The conciliator facilitates discussion and helps the parties reach a mutually agreeable resolution.
- Conciliation is typically completed within 30 days.
- A settlement agreement signed in conciliation is legally binding.
- Cost of conciliation: Rs. 2,000 for claims up to Rs. 10 lakhs; Rs. 5,000 for claims between Rs. 10 lakhs and Rs. 50 lakhs.

**Arbitration Process:**
- If conciliation fails, the investor may proceed to arbitration.
- An arbitration award is final and binding and may be enforced as a court decree.
- Arbitration is typically completed within 60 days for claims up to Rs. 25 lakhs, and 90 days for larger claims.

### 4.2 Obligations of Intermediaries in ODR

Intermediaries must:
- Participate in SEBI-mandated ODR proceedings within the timelines specified.
- Provide full cooperation and all relevant documents to the conciliator or arbitrator.
- Accept and implement ODR awards within 30 days of the award date.
- Failure to implement an ODR award is treated as contempt of the SEBI mechanism and may result in suspension of trading privileges.

## 5. Dispute Types and Typical Timelines

The following categories of disputes are commonly received on SCORES, with the expected resolution timelines under SCORES 2.0:

| Dispute Category | Typical Intermediary Resolution Time | Escalation Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Trade execution disputes (wrong price, quantity) | 7-10 days | Low (40% rate) |
| Margin shortfall / auto square-off disputes | 10-15 days | Medium (55% rate) |
| Fund transfer delays or incorrect credits/debits | 5-7 days | Low (30% rate) |
| Securities transfer disputes (pledge, transfer) | 15-21 days | High (65% rate) |
| Unauthorised transactions | 10-15 days | High (70% rate) |
| KYC or account modification disputes | 5-10 days | Low (25% rate) |

## 6. Special Provisions for Vulnerable Investors

SEBI has introduced special protections for vulnerable investor categories on SCORES:

### 6.1 Senior Citizens

Complaints from investors above 70 years of age are automatically tagged as "Senior Citizen Complaint" on SCORES and must be resolved within **15 calendar days** instead of the standard 21 days. The Nodal Officer must personally review all senior citizen complaints before responding.

### 6.2 Differently-Abled Investors

Complaints from investors with declared physical or cognitive disabilities must be handled with additional care. These investors may request assistance from SEBI's Investor Services Center (ISC) to file or follow up on their complaints.

### 6.3 Claims Following Death of Account Holder

Complaints regarding transmission of securities after the death of an account holder are expedited under SCORES. If the intermediary fails to process a valid transmission request within 30 days, the complainant (nominee or legal heir) may file a SCORES complaint and the intermediary must resolve within 10 days.

## 7. SEBI Enforcement Actions for SCORES Non-Compliance

SEBI monitors intermediary performance on SCORES through a real-time dashboard. Enforcement actions for SCORES non-compliance include:

- **Warning Letter:** For first-time failure to respond within prescribed timelines.
- **Advisory:** For patterns of inadequate quality responses.
- **Monetary Penalty:** Up to Rs. 1 lakh per complaint instance of wilful non-compliance.
- **Suspension of Registration:** For persistent refusal to engage with SCORES or implement ODR awards.
- **Public Disclosure:** SEBI maintains a public list of intermediaries with high complaint volumes and unresolved complaints, updated monthly on SEBI's website.

## 8. SEBI Investor Services

In addition to SCORES, SEBI provides the following investor services:

- **SEBI Investor Portal (investor.sebi.gov.in):** Educational resources, complaint filing, and status tracking.
- **Toll-Free Helpline (1800-22-7575 / 1800-266-7575):** Available Monday to Friday, 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
- **SEBI Regional Offices:** Located in Mumbai (HQ), Delhi, Chennai, Kolkata, Ahmedabad, Bangalore, Chandigarh, Jaipur, and Lucknow.

---

## 9. Conclusion

SCORES 2.0 represents SEBI's commitment to ensuring that every investor complaint receives a fair, timely, and substantive response. By establishing clear timelines (21 days at Level 1, 21 days at Level 2), by demanding quality over generic responses, and by integrating ODR for unresolved disputes, SEBI has created a comprehensive grievance ecosystem. The most important metric for any intermediary is not just resolving complaints on SCORES but preventing complaints from arising in the first place through high standards of service, transparency, and client communication.
