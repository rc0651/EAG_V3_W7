# SEBI Circular on Eligibility Framework for Retail Investors in Equity Derivatives

**Circular Reference:** SEBI/HO/MRD-1/DOP/P/CIR/2024/23
**Date:** March 5, 2024
**Issued By:** Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI)
**Applicable To:** All Stock Exchanges, Clearing Corporations, Stock Brokers, Depository Participants

---

## 1. Background and Objective

A significant proportion of retail investors who trade in equity derivatives (Futures and Options, or F&O) incur losses. A SEBI study released in January 2023 analysed the trading data of approximately 4.5 million individual investors who traded in equity F&O during FY2021-22. Key findings of the study included:

- 89% of individual traders in equity F&O incurred net losses during the period.
- The average net loss per loss-making trader was Rs. 1.1 lakhs for the year.
- Despite significant losses, many traders continued trading, with loss-making traders accounting for 72% of total turnover in equity F&O.
- A disproportionate share of losses was borne by younger, less experienced, and lower-income retail investors.

These findings indicated that a substantial portion of retail investors who trade in equity derivatives are financially ill-suited for the high risks involved in derivatives trading, including leverage, time decay, and gap risk. SEBI concluded that robust eligibility criteria were necessary to ensure that only investors with adequate financial standing and knowledge gain access to equity derivatives trading.

This circular establishes the eligibility framework that stock brokers must apply before activating equity derivatives trading for a retail client.

---

## 2. Definition of Retail Investor for Purposes of This Circular

For the purpose of this circular, a "retail investor" means any individual investor who is not:

- A Foreign Institutional Investor (FII) or Foreign Portfolio Investor (FPI) registered with SEBI
- A Category III Alternative Investment Fund (AIF)
- A proprietary trading desk of a registered broker
- A mutual fund or insurance company

All individual investors, regardless of their income or wealth, are classified as retail investors for the purpose of this circular unless they qualify for the "eligible non-institutional trader" status described in Section 4 below.

---

## 3. Minimum Eligibility Criteria for Retail Access to Equity Derivatives

With effect from October 1, 2024, stock brokers must verify and document that a client meets the following minimum criteria before activating the equity derivatives segment (both futures and options) in the client's trading account:

### 3.1 Income Requirement

The client must have declared and documented income of at least Rs. 5,00,000 (Rupees Five Lakhs) per annum from all sources. Acceptable documentation includes:

- Latest Income Tax Return (ITR) for the most recent assessment year
- Form 16 or salary certificate from employer for the current financial year
- Bank statements for the preceding 12 months showing regular income credits of at least Rs. 5 lakhs in aggregate
- Audited financial statements for self-employed clients or business owners

Income declared at account opening must be updated at least once every two years. If the client's income falls below the threshold at the time of renewal, the derivatives segment must be deactivated.

### 3.2 Net Worth Requirement (Alternative to Income Requirement)

In lieu of the income requirement, a client may qualify for equity derivatives trading if they have:

- Net worth of at least Rs. 10,00,000 (Rupees Ten Lakhs)

Net worth includes the value of all financial assets (equity, fixed deposits, mutual funds, etc.) minus all liabilities (loans, credit card outstanding, etc.). Real estate and illiquid assets may be included with supporting valuation documentation, subject to a maximum real estate contribution of 50% of total net worth.

### 3.3 Trading Experience Requirement

In addition to either the income or net worth criterion above, the client must also meet at least one of the following experience criteria:

**Option A — Prior Cash Segment Experience:**
The client must have an active demat and trading account with any SEBI-registered broker for at least 6 months, with at least 10 trades executed in the equity cash segment during this period.

**Option B — Financial Market Knowledge Certification:**
The client must have passed the NISM Series VIII (Equity Derivatives) examination or any equivalent examination approved by SEBI within the preceding 3 years. A passing score certificate must be uploaded to the KYC platform.

**Option C — Professional Qualification:**
The client holds a professional degree in finance, accounting, or economics (CA, CFA, MBA Finance, or equivalent) from a recognised institution.

A new client who has never traded in any segment cannot directly access equity derivatives. They must first trade in the equity cash segment for at least 6 months.

## 4. Enhanced Due Diligence for Derivatives Access

Before activating the derivatives segment, the broker must complete the following steps:

### 4.1 Risk Disclosure and Consent

The broker must provide the client with:

- SEBI's prescribed investor awareness document on the risks of F&O trading, including statistics on retail investor losses in derivatives.
- A broker-specific product suitability assessment noting the client's risk appetite and derivatives experience.
- A declaration by the client acknowledging that:
  - They understand the risks of derivatives trading including potential losses exceeding the initial investment
  - They meet the minimum eligibility criteria
  - They will not use borrowed funds (other than as expressly permitted by SEBI margin rules) for derivatives trading

The client must sign (digitally or physically) the risk disclosure and consent document. The broker must retain this document for at least 5 years.

### 4.2 Segment Activation Audit Trail

The broker must maintain an audit trail of the segment activation process including:

- Date of application by the client
- Documents submitted
- Verification steps completed
- Name and designation of the compliance officer who approved activation
- Date of activation

## 5. Restrictions on Index Options for New Retail Clients

### 5.1 Graduated Access to Weekly Expiry Contracts

SEBI has observed that short-duration weekly expiry index options contracts carry disproportionately high risk for retail investors due to rapid time decay. For retail clients who are newly approved for equity derivatives (within their first 90 days of derivatives trading):

- Trading in weekly expiry index options (expiry within 7 days) is restricted to buying options only.
- Selling (writing) options is not permitted in weekly expiry contracts for new retail clients.
- The restriction is automatically lifted after 90 days from the date of first derivatives trade.

This graduated access framework ensures that new retail derivatives traders are not immediately exposed to unlimited loss potential from option selling before they gain adequate experience.

### 5.2 Maximum Position Limits for Retail Clients

In addition to SEBI's standard position limits, brokers may (and are encouraged to) impose additional client-level position limits for retail clients based on the client's declared income, net worth, and experience. Specifically:

- The maximum open interest in derivatives for any single retail client must not exceed 5 times the client's declared annual income or 50% of the client's declared net worth, whichever is lower.
- Brokers who allow retail clients to take positions beyond these internal limits must be able to demonstrate, in any SEBI inspection, that adequate enhanced due diligence was conducted.

---

## 6. Review and Renewal of Derivatives Access

### 6.1 Annual Review

Brokers must review the eligibility of all retail clients for derivatives access at least once every two years. The review must verify:

- That the client's income or net worth still meets the minimum thresholds.
- That KYC documents are current and unexpired.
- That the client's risk profile has not changed materially (e.g., no recent insolvency proceedings, adverse court orders, or credit events).

### 6.2 Deactivation on Non-Compliance

If a client does not submit updated income/net worth documentation when requested, or if the updated documentation shows that the client no longer meets the minimum eligibility criteria, the broker must deactivate the derivatives segment within 30 days of the documentation deadline.

Deactivation of the derivatives segment for non-compliance with eligibility does not require SEBI approval but must be reported to the exchange in the monthly compliance report.

---

## 7. Obligations of Exchanges and Clearing Corporations

Stock exchanges and clearing corporations must:

- Maintain a centralised database of retail clients approved for derivatives trading, updated daily by member brokers.
- Conduct periodic audits of member broker compliance with this framework.
- Issue show-cause notices to member brokers where audit findings reveal material non-compliance with eligibility verification requirements.
- Provide SEBI with quarterly data on the number of retail clients approved for derivatives trading, categorised by income band and experience level.

---

## 8. Grandfathering of Existing Clients

Retail clients who already have an active derivatives segment as of the effective date of this circular (October 1, 2024) are grandfathered and do not need to re-apply for derivatives access. However, at the time of their next periodic KYC renewal (which must occur at least once every two years), they must submit income or net worth documentation as required by this circular. If at that point they do not meet the eligibility criteria, their derivatives access will be reviewed in consultation with the client.

---

## 9. Conclusion

SEBI's eligibility framework for retail investors in equity derivatives is designed to balance financial market access with investor protection. By ensuring that only clients with adequate financial standing, knowledge, and experience trade in high-risk derivatives products, SEBI aims to reduce the systemic social cost of retail investor losses in F&O markets. Brokers who diligently implement this framework not only comply with regulatory requirements but also build more sustainable client relationships based on appropriate product suitability.
